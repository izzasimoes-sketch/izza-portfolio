I
The Beginning

The
Beginning

There's a small city in the sertão of Paraíba, Brazil,

where the sun is relentless and the imagination runs free.

That's where I was born.

No design school. No roadmap.

Just a child with too many ideas and not enough paper.

I drew on everything. I built worlds in my head.

I made friends everywhere — different ages, different backgrounds,

different realities. I didn't know it yet,

but I was already doing design.


The internet? It arrived around 2000 — dial-up.

You'd sit there listening to the modem connect,

hoping the line wouldn't drop before the page loaded.

I learned patience before I learned to type.

Turns out, that's been more useful than any software.


2012.

Before I had a degree, I had a brand.

Pop Arretado — t-shirts, art direction, visual identity.

Founded from scratch. Built with instinct.


SEBRAE-PB recognized it as a Case of Success.

Universities used it in academic papers.

I didn't know at the time. I was just making things.


2013.

I enrolled in Graphic Design at Estácio.

Not to start — to formalize what had already begun.

the world opened up

II
The Spark

The
Spark

2015.

I joined StartPB — my first real contact

with the startup ecosystem.

The brand was already running. Now I had a framework to think bigger.

2016.

I graduated from Estácio.

The degree finally caught up with the practice.


2017.

tv2u International. UI Designer Jr.

They sent me to England for a full year of immersion.


That's where I met Alan Boyd —

the man who created PowerPoint.

And several other pieces of software you've used your entire life.

We sat in the same room and talked about ideas.

with Alan Boyd, England · 2017

I came back as Product Owner.

Project management. Agile. Marketing strategy.

Different hat, same company, same hunger.


2018.

OvermediaCast, São Paulo. Product Owner.

Storyboard. Roteiro. Inside sales. Project management.

A different kind of product. A different kind of thinking.


I was learning that design was never just design.

It was always about what happens next.

III
The Road

The
Road

2019.

I left OvermediaCast and went independent.


Freelance designer — graphic design, UI, identity.

Real clients. Real deadlines. No one to hide behind.

Just the work, and what you make of it.


I played guitar on street corners in between.

Moved through cities. Collected perspectives.

The kind of patience that only comes from

having nothing but time and a sketchbook.

guitar · streets
somewhere between a bus and a stage
traveling light
always moving · 2019–2021

2021.

Inovenow. My first proper UI role inside a team.

Design Thinking in the morning,

prototypes by end of day.

I learned how a product actually ships —

and how a team breathes together.


2022.

Deploy UX. I joined as UX/UI Designer.

Six months later — Head of User Experience.


That same year: Studio Nave.

Independent consultant. Still running today.

Working across sectors, building the muscle of adaptation.


Some people need a decade to get here.

I was building two careers at once.

IV
Today

Today

2022.

I moved to Porto.

Something clicked.


2023.

UZU Home — a real estate startup

with a vision and no product.

No name. No design system. No CRM. No brand.


That was my brief on day one.

UZU Home · Porto, 2024

I joined as Product Designer.

Ten months later — Senior Global Product Manager.

Nine months after that — CPO.


Not because I asked for it.

Because the work made it obvious.


Today I work at the intersection of

design, technology, and artificial intelligence.


I build products from zero:

from the first workshop to the last line of code.

I name companies. I design systems.

I use AI as a co-pilot, not a shortcut.


The girl from the sertão never stopped learning.

She just learned faster than everyone expected.
