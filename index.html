I
The Beginning

The
Beginning

There's a small city in the sertão of Paraíba, Brazil,

where the sun is relentless and the imagination runs free.

That's where I was born.

No design school. No roadmap.

Just a child with too many ideas and not enough paper.

I drew on everything. I built worlds in my head.

I made friends everywhere — different ages, different backgrounds,

different realities. I didn't know it yet,

but I was already doing design.


The internet? It arrived around 2000 — dial-up.

You'd sit there listening to the modem connect,

hoping the line wouldn't drop before the page loaded.

I learned patience before I learned to type.

Turns out, that's been more useful than any software.

II
The Spark

The
Spark

2015.

I joined a program called StartPB —

and that's where I first touched the world of startups.

E-commerce was just beginning. I was learning in real time.

The t-shirt brand idea was already in my head.

StartPB gave it a foundation.

2016.

I graduated. I opened the brand.

T-shirts. Graphic design. Real things, finally.


2017.

A startup called Tv2U brought me to England

for three months of full immersion.


That's where I met Alan Boyd —

the man who created PowerPoint.

And several other pieces of software you've used your entire life.

We sat in the same room and talked about ideas.

with Alan Boyd, England · 2017

That trip taught me something important:

the world is much smaller than we think,

and much more accessible than we're told.


I traveled. I played guitar on street corners.

Took courses. Crossed time zones. Made things.


What I got in return:

resilience, patience, and the ability to stay calm

when everything is on fire.


Which, in startups, is always useful.

Today

Today

I work at the intersection of design, technology,

and artificial intelligence.


Since 2017, I've been specialising in startups —

early-stage companies that need someone who can

hold the vision and build the thing at the same time.


I build products from zero — from the first workshop

to the last line of code.


I name companies. I design systems.

I lead technical migrations.

I use AI as a co-pilot, not a shortcut.


My skills go way beyond the technical.

That's exactly what makes the difference.

Long Road

There were years with no plan.

I moved alone. Different countries.

No safety net. No backup plan.

Not because I had to.

can build anything.
