I
The Beginning

The
Beginning

There's a small city in the sertão of Paraíba.

Relentless sun. Fertile imagination.

That's where I was born.

No design school. No mentor. No tutorial.

Just a girl who drew on everything she could find.

Notebooks. Church bulletins. The backs of receipts.

Not copies. Worlds. Characters. Systems.

Things she hadn't seen yet — but somehow knew should exist.


I was self-taught before I knew that word.

Then came the computers. I wanted my worlds to move.

I taught myself Photoshop. Then Illustrator.

Pixel by pixel — before anyone told me how.


2012.

I took the imagination off the walls.

Pop Arretado — shirts, art direction, full visual identity.

Built from zero. No brief. No budget. No safety net.

SEBRAE-PB called it a Case of Success. I was just making things.


2013.

I enrolled in Graphic Design at Estácio.

Not to start. To finally give a name to what I'd been doing all along.

the world opened up

II
The Spark

The
Spark

2015.

I joined StartPB — first contact with the startup world.

I'd been building without the vocabulary. Now I had the language.

2016.

I graduated from Estácio.

The paper finally caught up with the practice.


2017.

tv2u International. UI Designer Jr.

They gave me three months in England. Full immersion.


Plot twist: I ended up in the same room as Alan Boyd —

the man who created PowerPoint.

We talked about ideas as if we'd always known each other.

with Alan Boyd, England · 2017

I came back as Product Owner.

Back in Brazil — São Paulo.

Still with tv2u, now leading the Brazilian operations.


2018.

OvermediaCast. My first real encounter with AI.

Personalized interactive videos for brands you already know —

Randstad, Bradesco, Liberty Seguros, Ericsson, and others.

My job: write the scripts that made people act.

Technology as the engine. Persuasion as the fuel.


I didn't know it yet —

but AI had just become my co-pilot for life.

III
The Road

The
Road

2019.

I left everything and went independent.

No safety net. Real clients. Real consequences.

Just the work, and what you make of it.


2020.

Unfortunately, the world stopped.

But I'd always been independent — I turned the hours into something.

Studying every day. Making contacts online.

Trying to keep relationships alive through a screen.

Hard years for anyone with an entrepreneurial streak.

But every hard year teaches you something the good years never will.


2021.

Inovenow. First product team. Real sprint culture.

I learned how a product actually ships —

and how a team breathes under pressure.


2022.

I was already working remotely from Porto.

Earning less. Working five times harder.

Six client projects at the same time —

Deploy UX, Studio Nave, and everything in between.

I still found time to play guitar on the street to cover the bills.

Pressure either breaks you or builds you.

IV
Today

Today

2022.

I moved to Porto. Something shifted.

Not just the timezone. Everything.


2023.

UZU Home. A real estate startup with a bold vision.

And no product. No name. No brand. No design system.


That was my brief on day one.

UZU Home · Porto, 2024

I joined as Product Designer.

Ten months later — Senior Global Product Manager.

Nine months after that — CPO.


Not because I asked for it.

Because the work made it impossible to ignore.


Today I build at the intersection of design, technology, and AI.

I name companies. I ship code. I design systems.

I use Claude Code in production — and earned the cert to prove it.


That self-taught girl who drew on everything she could find —

now builds AI-powered products used by real people.

If you're building a startup that changes something —

I want to be in that room from day one.
